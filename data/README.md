This study uses the publicly available MIMIC-III critical care database, which contains de-identified longitudinal electronic health records of ICU patients. We focus on a cohort of sepsis patients and construct patient trajectories based on the first three treatment decision points. Clinical states are represented using 43 features, including demographics, laboratory results, vital signs, and intake/output events. Treatment actions consist of discretized intravenous fluid and vasopressor dosages, and outcomes are measured using the Sequential Organ Failure Assessment (SOFA) score. Processed datasets are provided in CSV format with accompanying feature documentation.

MIMIC-III database is accessible through the PhysioNet platform. To access the MIMIC-III dataset, follow these steps:
1.	Create a PhysioNet account and become a credentialed user.
2. 	Complete the required human subjects research training (CITI “Data or Specimens Only Research” course).
3.	Sign the PhysioNet data use agreement (DUA) for the MIMIC-III database.
4.	Upon approval, access and download the MIMIC-III v1.4 dataset through the PhysioNet platform.
